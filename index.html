An overview of our Technology Student Association chapter

Introduction

The Ithaca High School Technology Student Association—IHSTSA—was for a long time overshadowed by Ithaca High School’s other technology clubs. However, for reasons that no one entirely understands, IHSTSA’s membership exploded in September 2015. Though we have always done well at the New York State Competition, this year we needed a long paragraph to list our awards. Gone are the times when we sent only eight members to Nationals—this year, we will send 20 and not everyone will get to go.

Back home in Ithaca, we make up a significant fraction of Ithaca High School’s technology and science geeks:

(Making technology awesome)

We don’t use the motto of the National Technology Student Association, because we feel ours most accurately expresses what we do at Ithaca High School TSA. The decision to write it in a binary format was made to reflect our nerdiness. Nor do we conform to the National Technology Student Association in our creed: ours is secular and international in scope. We’ve been working up the courage to make a resolution at the national business meeting to change the National TSA motto to something with the positive attributes of ours.

The Technology Education holds an important place in our lives in the technical world. There is a need for the development of good attitudes concerning work, tools, materials, experimentation, and processes of industry. We will accept the responsibilities that are ours and the theories that are supported by proper evidence. We will explore on our own for safer, more effective methods of working and living. We will strive to be fun and respectful. Through our work, we will express our ideas to the best of our ability. In short, we will strive to make the world a better place in which to live.
